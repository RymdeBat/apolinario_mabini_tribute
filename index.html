Apolinario Mabini

Brain of the Revolution

Here's a time line of Apolinario Mabinini's life:

  • 1864 - Born in Barrio Talaga in Tanauan, Batangas
  • 1881 - He received a scholarship from Colegio de San Juan de Letran in Manila
  • 1892 - Mabini became a member of La Liga Filipina, which was being resuscitated after the arrest of its founder José Rizal
  • 1894 - A year after receiving his Bachiller en Artes with highest honors and the title Professor of Latin from Letran, he moved on to University of Santo Tomas, where he received his law degree
  • 1895 - Mabini was struck by polio
  • 1896 - The disease gradually incapacitated him, lost the use of both his legs
  • 1898 - Mabini remained in ill health for a considerable time. He was seeking the curative properties of the hot springs in Los Baños, Laguna when Emilio Aguinaldo sent for him, asking him to serve as advisor to the revolution
  • 1899 - He continued to serve as the chief adviser for General Aguinaldo after the Philippine Declaration of Independence on June 12. He drafted decrees and edited the constitution for the First Philippine Republic, including the framework of the revolutionary government which was implemented in Malolos
  • 1903 - Mabini returned to the Philippines after agreeing to take the Oath of Allegiance to the United States. On the same year, he died at the age of 38 after consuming an unpasteurized and contaminated carabao milk.

"The Spaniards underestimated Mabini primarily because he was a cripple. Had they known of his intellectual perspicacity, they would have killed him earlier. The Americans did not. They were aware of his superior intelligence, his tenacity when he faced them in negotiations for autonomy and ceasefire."

--National Artist for Literature F. Sionil Jose
